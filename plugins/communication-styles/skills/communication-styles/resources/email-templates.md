# Email Templates by Communication Style

## Template Structure

Each style requires different emphasis in:
1. Subject line
2. Opening
3. Body structure
4. Call-to-action
5. Closing

---

## AMIABLE Templates

### Template 1: Request for Input

```
Subject: Seeking Your Valuable Input on [Project Name]

Hi [Name],

I hope you and your team are doing well. I wanted to reach out because I really value your perspective on [topic].

We've been discussing [project/initiative] with the team, and there's some interest in moving forward. Before we do, I'd love to hear your thoughts and make sure this feels right for everyone involved.

KEY CONSIDERATIONS:
- How this could help our teams
- Impact on your organization
- Concerns we should address together

Would you be open to a conversation about this? I want to make sure we're all aligned and comfortable with the direction.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 2: Proposal for Approval

```
Subject: Team Collaboration Opportunity - [Project Name]

Dear [Name],

I hope this finds you well. I wanted to share an idea that I think could really benefit our teams and strengthen collaboration across the organization.

Several people have mentioned [problem/challenge], and after discussing with colleagues, there seems to be strong consensus that [solution] could help.

WHY THIS MATTERS TO OUR TEAMS:
- Improves work-life balance
- Reduces manual effort by [X%]
- Strengthens team collaboration
- Supports our shared goals

I've attached a one-pager that outlines the approach. I'd really value your input and want to make sure this aligns with your team's needs and priorities.

Could we find time to discuss this together? I want to ensure we have your support and address any concerns.

Warmly,
[Your Name]
```

## Email Checklist by Style

### Before Sending to AMIABLE:
- [ ] Personal opening (not diving straight to business)
- [ ] Mentions team/relationships
- [ ] Asks for their opinion/input
- [ ] Allows time for consensus
- [ ] Warm, collaborative tone
- [ ] Avoids rushing or demanding

### Before Sending to EXPRESSIVE:
- [ ] Enthusiastic tone
- [ ] Story or vision included
- [ ] Recognition/appreciation mentioned
- [ ] Visually appealing if possible
- [ ] Asks for their ideas
- [ ] Details in attachments (not body)

### Before Sending to ANALYTIC:
- [ ] Data and evidence included
- [ ] Methodology explained
- [ ] Detailed documentation attached
- [ ] Precise and accurate language
- [ ] Professional, business-focused
- [ ] Allows time for analysis

### Before Sending to DRIVER:
- [ ] Bottom line in first sentence
- [ ] Results and ROI quantified
- [ ] Specific decision/action needed
- [ ] Clear deadline included
- [ ] Options with recommendation
- [ ] Brief and to the point (no rambling)

## Emergency Email Rewrites

**Original (style-neutral):**
"I wanted to follow up on our conversation about the project. I think we should consider moving forward with the implementation. Let me know your thoughts."

**This is weak for ALL styles. Here's how to strengthen:**

**AMIABLE rewrite:**
"Thank you for the great conversation yesterday! I really valued hearing your perspective and your team's concerns. After reflecting on what you shared, I think moving forward could help address the challenges we discussed - but I want to make sure this feels right to you and your team. Would you be open to discussing this further?"

**EXPRESSIVE rewrite:**
"I loved our conversation yesterday! Your ideas really sparked my excitement about the possibilities here. I think we could create something amazing if we move forward with implementation. What do you think about turning this into a showcase project? I'd love to hear your creative ideas!"

**ANALYTIC rewrite:**
"Following up on our discussion yesterday. Based on the analysis we reviewed (attached detailed documentation), the data supports moving forward with implementation. The methodology shows 87% confidence in projected outcomes. Please review the attached risk assessment and implementation plan. I'm available to answer questions about the analysis."

**DRIVER rewrite:**
"Quick follow-up from yesterday: Recommend we move forward with implementation. Results: $200K savings, 60-day timeline, low risk. Need your approval by Thursday to hit target dates. Yes or no?"

## Pro Tips

1. **When in doubt about style:** Lead with DRIVER + ANALYTIC (task-focused) in business contexts
2. **For unknown recipients:** Use multi-layer structure (all four styles in sequence)
3. **For urgent matters:** Always include DRIVER elements (bottom line, deadline, decision needed)
4. **For sensitive topics:** Always include AMIABLE elements (personal connection, consensus)
5. **Watch response patterns:** If they reply in their style, mirror it in your next email
6. **Subject line is critical:** 50% of engagement happens before email is opened
7. **First sentence matters most:** Front-load most important information by style
8. **Attachments by style:**
   - Amiables: Implementation impact on teams
   - Expressives: Visual one-pagers with stories
   - Analytics: Detailed analysis and methodology
   - Drivers: One-page executive summary with ROI

Remember: The goal is not manipulation but removing communication barriers to enable effective collaboration.
