### EMOTIONAL TONE (Advanced)

**Select the tone that best serves your narrative:**

- [ ] **Surprised**: Highlight unexpected results that challenge assumptions
  - Use when: Results contradict expectations or conventional wisdom
  - Example: "Despite reducing marketing spend 20%, lead generation increased 34%"

- [ ] **Inspired**: Paint a vision of what's possible
  - Use when: Presenting transformative opportunities or early wins
  - Example: "This positions us to dominate the SMB segment within 18 months"

- [ ] **Reassured**: Demonstrate control and stability during uncertainty
  - Use when: Navigating challenges, maintaining confidence during setbacks
  - Example: "Despite Q2 challenges, underlying metrics remain healthy and we're on track"

- [ ] **Concerned**: Highlight risks requiring immediate attention (use sparingly)
  - Use when: Urgent issues need executive prioritization
  - Example: "At current trajectory, our unit economics become unprofitable in Q4"

**How will you embed this tone?**

## Narrative Quality Check

Before finalizing, review:

- [ ] Does WHAT clearly state current state and connect to CEO priority?
- [ ] Does WHY provide data-driven, depersonalized root cause analysis?
- [ ] Does NEXT include specific, actionable recommendations with outcomes and timeline?
- [ ] Is the decision required explicit and clear?
- [ ] Have I eliminated jargon or defined necessary terms?
- [ ] Does this narrative tell a story (not just present data)?
- [ ] Would an executive from a different department understand the strategic significance?
- [ ] Have I embedded appropriate emotional tone?
- [ ] Is this focused on the decision, not just information sharing?
