  - Will they accept accountability?

## Power Source Inventory

Check all sources of power you have available:

- [ ] **Positional power** - Authority from your role
  - What authority do you have?
  - What can you approve/deny/block?

- [ ] **Expert power** - Technical or domain expertise
  - What expertise gives you credibility?
  - Who respects your technical judgment?

- [ ] **Resource power** - Control over budget, headcount, or assets
  - What resources do you control?
  - What resources do they need from you?

- [ ] **Relationship power** - Network and alliances
  - Who can you call on for support?
  - Who trusts your judgment?

- [ ] **Information power** - Access to critical data or insights
  - What information do you have that they need?
  - What insights can you provide leadership?

- [ ] **Process power** - Control over workflows or approvals
  - What processes do you control?

## Alternative Options Analysis

Before proceeding, consider alternatives:

### Option 1: Verbal Diplomacy (One More Try)
- [ ] Could this still work?
- [ ] Have I truly exhausted diplomatic options?
- [ ] Is there a new angle I haven't tried?

### Option 2: Escalation to Superior
- [ ] Would escalation be more effective?
- [ ] Do I lack sufficient power?
- [ ] Would my superior handle it better?

### Option 3: Structural/Process Changes
- [ ] Could we change the process to prevent this?
- [ ] Would systemic changes be better than individual action?
- [ ] Can we remove the opportunity for this behavior?

### Option 4: Accept and Monitor
- [ ] Is the damage truly significant enough?
- [ ] Could this resolve itself?
- [ ] Should I monitor longer before acting?
