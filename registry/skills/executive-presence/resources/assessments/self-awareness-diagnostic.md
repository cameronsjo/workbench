# Self-Awareness Diagnostic Assessment

Evaluate your current level of self-awareness regarding how others perceive you.

## The Self-Awareness Gap

**Research finding:** 95% of people believe they are self-aware, but only 15% actually are.

This diagnostic helps you assess where you fall on the self-awareness spectrum.

## Scoring Interpretation

### 120-150: High Self-Awareness

You have exceptional self-awareness and brand intentionality. You understand how others perceive you, your impact, and actively manage your brand. Continue refining and evolving your brand based on market needs.

**Recommended focus:** Brand optimization and Parts 2-3 of executive presence (Impressions and Impact)

---

### 90-119: Moderate Self-Awareness

You have good self-awareness but opportunities to deepen understanding and intentionality. You may have blind spots or gaps between intention and impact.

**Recommended focus:** Conduct brand discovery conversations, develop anti-brand list, create brand promise

---

### 60-89: Developing Self-Awareness

You are beginning to develop self-awareness but significant gaps exist. Your brand may be more accidental than intentional.

**Recommended focus:** Start brand observation journal, conduct brand discovery conversations, complete zone of distinction analysis

---

### Below 60: Low Self-Awareness

You are likely in the 85% of people who overestimate their self-awareness. This is common and addressable through structured discovery.

**Recommended focus:** Complete full brand discovery process starting with observation journal and brand discovery conversations
