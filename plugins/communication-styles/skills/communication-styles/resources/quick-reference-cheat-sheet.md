# Communication Styles Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

## The Four Social Styles

```
                      RELATIONSHIP
                           ↑
                AMIABLE  |  EXPRESSIVE
           (People)      |      (Ideas)
      ASK ←────────────────────────────→ TELL
           (Process)     |     (Results)
                ANALYTIC |    DRIVER
                           ↓
                         TASK
```

## Style Profiles at a Glance

| Dimension | AMIABLE | EXPRESSIVE | ANALYTIC | DRIVER |
|-----------|---------|------------|----------|--------|
| **Focus** | People | Ideas | Process | Results |
| **Tagline** | "Let me discuss this with my team" | "Here are my ideas about this" | "Let me think how it could work" | "Let's take action on this" |
| **Interested in** | Human connection | Ideas and possibilities | Facts and data | Action and outcomes |
| **They seek** | Consensus | Recognition | Accuracy | Results |
| **Decision pattern** | Slow and thoughtful | Fast and spontaneous | Slow and systematic | Decisive and results-focused |
| **Want to save** | Relationships | Effort | Face | Time |
| **Ask questions about** | Why | Who | How | What |

## 20-Second Diagnostic

**Step 1:** Relationship vs. Task?
- Do they start meetings with personal talk or dive into business?
- Do they prioritize team impact or bottom-line results?

**Step 2:** Ask vs. Tell?
- Do they ask questions or make statements?
- Are they reserved or assertive?

**Result:**
- Relationship + Ask = AMIABLE
- Relationship + Tell = EXPRESSIVE
- Task + Ask = ANALYTIC
- Task + Tell = DRIVER

## Engagement Strategies

### AMIABLE
**DO:**
- Connect personally before business
- Ask for opinions and listen
- Talk about holistic concepts
- Give time to build consensus
- Be warm and collaborative

**DON'T:**
- Rush into business
- Be domineering or pushy
- Force quick decisions
- Ignore team concerns

**OPEN WITH:** "How are you and your team doing?"

**CLOSE WITH:** "Does this feel right to you and your team?"

---

### EXPRESSIVE
**DO:**
- Provide warm, friendly environment
- Tell specific stories
- Use emotional appeals
- Recognize their contributions
- Make it exciting and visual

**DON'T:**
- Be curt or cold
- Control the conversation
- Drown in details
- Be boring or overly formal

**OPEN WITH:** "I'm excited to share this with you!"

**CLOSE WITH:** "What ideas do you have to make this even better?"

---

### ANALYTIC
**DO:**
- Prepare case with data
- Be accurate and realistic
- Use detailed models
- Stick to business
- Give time to analyze

**DON'T:**
- Be casual or loud
- Push hard for deadlines
- Be disorganized
- Make claims without evidence

**OPEN WITH:** "I've analyzed the data and here's what I found..."

**CLOSE WITH:** "I'll send you the detailed documentation to review."

---

### DRIVER
**DO:**
- Be clear, specific, brief
- Stick to business
- Use concrete examples
- Focus on results and ROI
- Respect their time

**DON'T:**
- Go off topic
- Appear disorganized
- Miss deadlines
- Waste time with details

**OPEN WITH:** "Bottom line: this will save us $500K."

**CLOSE WITH:** "What's your decision?"

## Stress Responses

| Style | Under Stress | How to De-escalate |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **AMIABLE** | Acquiesce - Comply despite disagreement | Create safe space for honest concerns |
| **EXPRESSIVE** | Attack - Defend ideas aggressively, become critical | Let them vent, redirect to solutions |
| **ANALYTIC** | Avoid - Indecisiveness, evade people and deadlines | Provide clear deadline with rationale |
| **DRIVER** | Autocracy - Dictatorial, pushy, intimidating | Stand firm with facts, respect authority |

## Compatibility Matrix

```
           AMIABLE    EXPRESSIVE    ANALYTIC    DRIVER
AMIABLE      ✓✓          ✓            ~          ✗
EXPRESSIVE   ✓          ✓✓            ✗          ~
ANALYTIC     ~           ✗           ✓✓          ✓
DRIVER       ✗           ~            ✓          ✓✓
```

**Legend:**
- ✓✓ High compatibility (same style)
- ✓ Good compatibility (shared dimension)
- ~ Moderate tension (diagonal neighbors)
- ✗ High tension (opposite styles)

**Opposite Styles (Maximum Tension):**
- AMIABLE ↔ DRIVER (consensus vs. speed)
- EXPRESSIVE ↔ ANALYTIC (story vs. data)

## Email Subject Line Formulas

| Style | Formula | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **AMIABLE** | "Seeking [your/team] input on [topic]" | "Seeking your team's input on new process" |
| **EXPRESSIVE** | "[Exciting/Innovative] [opportunity/idea] - [topic]" | "Exciting innovation opportunity - Project X" |
| **ANALYTIC** | "[Analysis/Review] and recommendation for [topic]" | "Analysis and recommendation for platform upgrade" |
| **DRIVER** | "[Action/Decision] needed: [topic] - [impact]" | "Decision needed: Project X - $500K impact" |

## Meeting Opener Formulas

| Style | Formula | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **AMIABLE** | "How are [you/team]..." | "How are you and your team doing this week?" |
| **EXPRESSIVE** | "I'm excited to..." | "I'm excited to share this game-changing idea!" |
| **ANALYTIC** | "I've analyzed/prepared..." | "I've prepared a detailed analysis for our discussion." |
| **DRIVER** | "Bottom line:..." | "Bottom line: we need to decide on three options today." |

## Multi-Style Presentation Layer Structure

**Layer 1: DRIVER (15 seconds)**
- What's the decision?
- What's the impact?
- What's the timeline?

**Layer 2: EXPRESSIVE (30 seconds)**
- Why should they care?
- What's the vision?
- What's the story?

**Layer 3: ANALYTIC (2 minutes)**
- What's the data?
- What's the methodology?
- What's the evidence?

**Layer 4: AMIABLE (1 minute)**
- Who's involved?
- How does this help teams?
- What's the consensus?

## Quick Flexing Rules

**When communicating with:**

**AMIABLES**
- Slow down
- Connect personally
- Seek consensus
- Give time

**EXPRESSIVES**
- Add energy
- Tell stories
- Recognize contributions
- Be enthusiastic

**ANALYTICS**
- Provide data
- Be precise
- Give time to analyze
- Show methodology

**DRIVERS**
- Get to the point
- Focus on results
- Save their time
- Be decisive

## Red Flags by Style

**AMIABLE red flags:**
- Saying "yes" but not committing
- Avoiding eye contact during agreement
- "I'll think about it and discuss with team" (indefinitely)

**EXPRESSIVE red flags:**
- Becoming defensive or judgmental
- Personally attacking ideas
- Escalating emotionally

**ANALYTIC red flags:**
- Requesting endless additional analysis
- Avoiding meetings or decisions
- "I need more time to review" (repeatedly)

**DRIVER red flags:**
- Becoming dictatorial
- Interrupting and bulldozing
- Making unilateral decisions

## Emergency Flex Guide

**"They're not responding to my emails"**
- Check: Are you matching their style?
- Try: Rewrite email in their style format
- Amiable: Add personal connection
- Expressive: Add excitement and vision
- Analytic: Add data and documentation
- Driver: Cut to bottom line immediately

**"Meetings with them are always tense"**
- Check: Are you opposite styles?
- Amiable-Driver: Schedule decision deadlines
- Expressive-Analytic: Lead with data OR story first
- Practice: Their style in low-stakes situations

**"They agreed but nothing happened"**
- Amiable: They acquiesced without genuine buy-in
- Solution: Create safe space for honest concerns
- Ask directly: "What are your real concerns?"

**"They're attacking my ideas"**
- Expressive: Under stress, becoming defensive
- Solution: Let them vent, acknowledge emotions
- Redirect: "Let's channel this passion into solving the problem"

## One-Page Action Plan

1. **Diagnose** (20 seconds): Relationship vs. Task? Ask vs. Tell?
2. **Identify** their style: Amiable, Expressive, Analytic, or Driver
3. **Flex** your approach:
   - Use their power words
   - Match their decision speed
   - Address their primary interest
   - Avoid their tension factors
4. **Observe** their response and adjust in real-time
5. **Practice** the style that's hardest for you

**Remember:** You're not manipulating - you're removing communication barriers to enable effective collaboration.
